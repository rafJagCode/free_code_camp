Highlights of Albert Einstein's Scientific Contributions

  • 1905 - Annus Mirabilis (Miracle Year)
    • Published four groundbreaking papers in the "Annalen der Physik" journal.
    • Photoelectric Effect: Proposed that light is quantized, earning him the Nobel Prize in 1921.
    • Brownian Motion: Provided empirical evidence for the existence of atoms.
    • Special Theory of Relativity: Introduced the famous equation E=mcÂ².
    • Mass-Energy Equivalence: Demonstrated the interconversion of mass and energy.
  • 1915 - General Theory of Relativity
    • Presented the final form of the General Theory of Relativity, explaining gravity as the curvature of spacetime.
    • Confirmed by the 1919 solar eclipse, which observed the bending of light by gravity.
  • 1921 - Nobel Prize in Physics
    • Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933 - Emigration to the United States
    • Fled Nazi Germany and took a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
  • 1939 - Letter to President Roosevelt
    • Co-signed a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt, warning about the potential for Nazi Germany to develop atomic weapons.
    • Led to the establishment of the Manhattan Project.
  • 1952 - Presidency of Israel
    • Offered the presidency of Israel, which he declined.
